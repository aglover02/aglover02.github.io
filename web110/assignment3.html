Assignment 3 - Summary

Session 3 Search engines Activity 1: Compare search engines For this activity, I had to search for 'web search engine' on Google, Bing, Yahoo, and Ask, comparing results and deciding which search engine had the best user interface and most useful results. I also had to search for which search engine has the highest market share. I learned about the differences between the different search engines. Google is extremely simple. Bing has more going on with their background photo and news articles on the home page. Yahoo has even more going on with the news, the weather, games, and trends. Ask has multiple articles on its home page along with a question of the day. I believe Google provides the most useful results and is the easiest to use due to its simplicity. I also learned that Google has the highest market share with 91.61%. I can apply this to my school and work environment by being more selective of which search engines I will use for different things. Activity 2: Use search engines For this activity, I had to look up "search engine" "market share" with and without quotes to see the difference in results. I also compared results between searching on Wikiversity using site:wikiversity.org, and using the built-in search page. I also went over Georgetown's guidelines to identify the most reliable information in search results. I learned that doing an exact search for “search engine” and “market share” using quotes can improve the result accuracy by narrowing down to pages containing the exact phrase. I also learned that while searching Wikiversity, using the site: operator provides more targeted results compared to the built-in Special: Search page. I can apply this to my school and work environment in the future by being far more efficient while searching for information, along with being able to discern reliable vs unreliable websites.
